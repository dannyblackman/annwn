How We Speak

Knowledgeable but never condescending. We simplify complexity without dumbing it down. The expert friend who makes sustainability compliance feel manageable.

✓ Do
"Your CSRD report is 94% complete. Here's what's left." Clear, specific, action-oriented
✗ Don't
"Our cutting-edge AI-powered solution leverages advanced algorithms to streamline your compliance journey." Jargon-heavy, vague, overly corporate
✓ Do
"We spotted 3 gaps in your Scope 3 emissions data. Let's fix them." Helpful, direct, human
✗ Don't
"WARNING: Critical compliance failure detected. Immediate action required." Alarmist, impersonal, anxiety-inducing
✓ Do
"Nice progress — you've reduced emissions by 23% this quarter." Encouraging, specific, warm
✗ Don't
"The system has processed your data. Please review the output at your earliest convenience." Robotic, passive, impersonal
07

Image Style

Our photography is bright, human, and grounded. Real people in real workspaces — natural light, plants, and warmth. Professional without the stock-photo stiffness.

Bright, open workspaces with natural light
Genuine collaboration, not posed
Greenery as part of the environment
Teams working, not performing

Natural light

Always favour daylight. Window-lit scenes, soft shadows, warm tones. Avoid harsh flash or fluorescent overhead lighting.

Living spaces

Plants, wood, natural textures. Offices that feel alive. Show environments where people actually want to work — biophilic by default.

Real people

Professional, diverse, and candid. People should be engaged in actual work — not awkwardly grinning at the camera or pointing at screens.

Soft palette

Images should feel consistent with our colour palette. Muted greens, warm whites, soft tones. Light post-processing is fine — heavy filters are not.

In-the-moment

Capture work in progress — laptops open, whiteboards mid-session, coffee half-drunk. Energy and motion over static poses.

Aspirational, not fantasy

Show workplaces people recognise but slightly elevated. Clean desks, good light, happy teams — realistic but at its best.

Avoid

✗ Hands cupping a glowing globe or seedling
✗ Aerial views of deforestation or pollution
✗ Suited executives shaking hands in front of charts
✗ Wind turbines at golden hour (unless genuinely relevant)
✗ People high-fiving or jumping in celebration
✗ Overly saturated "eco green" colour grading
✗ Generic diverse teams around a conference table looking at camera
✗ AI-generated or heavily composited imagery
07b

Decorative Gradients

Soft gradient orbs and avatar backgrounds add depth and warmth. They should feel organic, never harsh — always blurred and subtly animated.

Background Orbs

Floating gradient orbs create atmospheric depth. Use sparingly on hero sections, title slides, and thank-you pages. Always apply heavy blur (80px+) and low opacity (5–25%).

Dark background

Avatar Gradients

When photos aren't available, use soft gradient backgrounds for avatar placeholders. Each should be unique using different palette combinations.
