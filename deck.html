02
Problem

Sustainability is a structurally constrained profession — and consultants currently pay the price

Sustainability has a broad remit: spanning every part of a business and many topics, but sits apart. No mandate, scattered data, poor tools, and an ever-shifting brief mean…

  • Slow and expensive

    The priority work is manual (low value and high cost) and enterprise clients expect more for less.

  • Knowledge doesn't accumulate

    Methodology and context leave with people. Every engagement starts from scratch.

  • No space for strategy

    Clients are consumed by compliance. True impact and value creation gets sidelined.

Consultants are seen as vendors, not partners. Budget cuts and regulatory retrenchment mean more pressure to deliver, tighter budgets, and less influence.

  • Knowledge System

  • Innovation Engine

    Costed strategic opportunities surfaced automatically across the portfolio. Board-ready energy efficiency case for the CFO. New business for the consultancy.

The more annwn is used, the more powerful it becomes.
